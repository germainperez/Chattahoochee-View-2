The Chattahoochee River National Water Trail

The water flowing out from the bottom of Lake Lanier remains about 50° Farenheit year-round and thus in warmer months the river can be shrowded in mist. It is a haven for trout fishermen, paddlers, and tubers.

In 2012 the U.S. Dept. of Interior named this stretch of river from Buford Dam to Atlanta its first National Water Trail.
